Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of things describes devices with sensors, processing ability, software and other technologies that connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the Internet or other communications networks. The Internet of things encompasses electronics, communication, and computer science engineering

Scope of IoT

The scope of IoT encompasses various sectors, including smart homes, healthcare, industrial applications, smart cities, retail, agriculture, energy management, transportation, environmental monitoring, and wearable technology. IoT devices and sensors enable data collection, connectivity, and automation to improve efficiency, productivity, and decision-making in these domains.

Applications

  • Smart Homes: Automation, security, energy management.
  • Healthcare: Remote patient monitoring, telemedicine, medication management.
  • Industrial IoT (IIoT): Predictive maintenance, asset tracking, process automation.
  • Smart Cities: Transportation systems, waste management, public safety.
  • Agriculture: Soil monitoring, crop health tracking, livestock management.
  • Transportation and Logistics: Vehicle tracking, fleet management, route optimization.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Air and water quality monitoring, natural habitat preservation.
